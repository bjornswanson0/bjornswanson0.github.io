Roots

Where I come from

Every story starts somewhere, and mine starts in Edina, Minnesota — a small town on the west side with a dream. I grew up there in the middle seat: older brother Gunnar ahead of me, little sister Karsten behind.

With a lineup like Gunnar, Bjorn, and Karsten you can guess the heritage — Scandinavian, with the temperament to match: understated, steady, happiest with a plan and a long day of work ahead. What I took from home wasn't complicated: show up early, do the work, take care of your people. I've been running on that operating system ever since.

The Journey

Chapters so far

  1. The early years

    Growing up in Edina between Gunnar and Karsten, I learned early how to compete for everything and share the credit. Somewhere in there the pattern was set: pick a thing, get quietly obsessive about it, get good.

  2. College & the brotherhood

    At the University of Minnesota I studied Management Information Systems at the Carlson School and joined Pi Kappa Alpha — where I found a group of guys who, years later, still block off a long weekend every summer to tee it up together. Some things you don't graduate from.

  3. New York City

    After school I moved to New York and landed in consulting. I fell for the city the way everyone warns you that you will — the pace, the corner delis, the feeling that everyone here is building something. The East Village is home, and the work sharpened into a focus: strategy, and lately, what AI changes about it.

  4. The next chapter

    Still being written. The short version: bigger problems, more ownership, and work that puts strategy and AI in the same sentence. More on that below.

Passions

What gets me going

Always learning

Every new client is a crash course in a new industry, and I wouldn't have it any other way. Lately the curriculum is AI — what it can do, what it changes, and how to put it to work.

Building

I like making things that get used: small apps that smooth out a week, tools that save an hour, this site. AI has collapsed the distance between an idea and a working version — I intend to keep closing it.

The daily session

I'm up early for the office; the training happens once the day's work is done. The gym is the keel of my week — the discipline everything else gets built on. The goal has never been bulk; it's being lean, strong, and nimble for a long time.

Now

Where I am today

Today I'm a Management Consultant at Accenture, working across financial services and retail — helping clients transform their digital platforms and put AI to work.

The through-line of my work is helping organizations decide — clearly, quickly, and with conviction — in a moment when AI is rewriting what's possible. I like the messy middle: where the analysis ends and someone has to make the call.

What's Next

Where I'm headed

Lately, nothing has me more energized than AI — not as a buzzword, but as a force multiplier for ambition and creativity. It has turned me into a builder: apps that make my week run smoother, a website for my corner deli, and whatever idea shows up next.

The goals underneath are simpler: become a better person, a better coworker, and a better friend. I believe positivity is the strongest force in this world — and the next few years are about proving it, at work and everywhere else.
